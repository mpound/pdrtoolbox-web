[Ar V] 13.07 µm/[Ar V] 7.91 µm Emissivity Ratio

pdrtpy ModelSet: wk2020

[Ar V] 13.07 µm/[Ar V] 7.91 µm Emissivity Ratio emitted from the ionized gas layer of a PDR as a function of the electron gas temperature Te and electron density ne. We assume that the line emission is in the optically thin limit so that the ratio of emissivities is given by the ratio of volume emissivity. For \({\rm Ar^{+2}}\) , and \({\rm Ar^{+4}}\) , we use CHIANTI ( Dere et al (1997); DelZanna et al. (2022)) using the default values for the A values and collision strengths. For \({\rm Fe^+}\) we substituted the default values in CHIANTI with Einstein A values from Deb & Hibbert (2011) and collision strengths from Smyth et al. (2019) . The emissivity ratios are found in the temperature range from \(T_e=10^3\) K to \(10^4\) K, and the density range from \(n_e = 10^2~{\rm cm^{-3}}\) to \(10^6~{\rm cm^{-3}}\).

The FITS file is available for download.
